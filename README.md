This repository contains my work for the **Mastercard Cybersecurity Virtual Experience Program** on [Forage](https://www.theforage.com/). The program simulates real-world tasks carried out by Mastercard's Security Awareness Team, focusing on phishing threat detection, simulation, and employee training.

## Tasks

### Task 1 — Design a phishing email simulation

**Objective:** Understand what phishing is and create a believable phishing simulation email to be used in an internal staff awareness campaign.

**What I did:**
- Analyzed an obvious fake phishing email and identified its weaknesses
- Redesigned it to be contextual, professional, and convincing
- Applied social engineering techniques such as urgency, authority, and masked hyperlinks

**Key concepts covered:**
- What phishing is and why it's dangerous
- Common traits of phishing emails (spoofed domains, urgency, credential harvesting)
- How organizations like Mastercard run internal phishing simulations

**Deliverable:** [`Phishing Email and Activity Progression`](./assets/Mastercard_Internship_Simulation_Activity_Progression.pdf)

---

### Task 2 — Interpret phishing simulation results

**Objective:** Analyze the results of the phishing simulation campaign, identify the most vulnerable teams, and create a training presentation to improve security awareness.

**What I did:**
- Interpreted phishing simulation data across 7 departments
- Identified HR (75% success rate) and Marketing (38% success rate) as the highest-risk teams
- Designed a security awareness training presentation targeted at vulnerable teams

**Key concepts covered:**
- Reading and interpreting phishing campaign metrics (open rate, click-through rate, success rate)
- Identifying patterns in employee behavior across departments
- Designing targeted security awareness training

**Deliverable:** [`Training Material for Phishing Program`](./assets/Phishing-Training-Presentation.pptx)
